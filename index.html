BREAKING NEWS: DONQUIXOTE BUYS TWITTER

Live updates Author: Doffy D. Simp

The WENP has just received reports that, as of this morning, Donquixote Doflamingo, billionaire King of Dressrosa and current Warlord, has just purchased the Social Media company, Twitter, for 44 Billion Beri.

The known Privateer and beloved Monarch has reportedly already let go of the former staff and replaced them with trusted insiders. Rumors of the to be announced replacements include new Chief of Infrastructure, Pica. Pica, longtime friend and business ally to Donquixote is an unconventional choice, for though his background is in infrastructure as the Chief of Operations Spade Executive of Donquixote Industries, it is of the physical variety. We’re not sure what building physical buildings will bring to the table when making decisions about digital infrastructure, but it will likely include a pivot to Web3, as Pica is an early investor in Flamincoin and NFTs.

That is not the most controversial choice however, as many have predicted that Donquixote is appointing Trebol, another longtime supporter and current Chief Financial Officer of Donquixote Industries (the beloved Head of Clubs) to oversee the company’s content moderation and user base. Trebol has made headlines over the last ten years numerous times for making adversarial public statements, not the least of which have been aimed at Donquixote’s own brother, who was killed in a drug bust (see next article) thirteen years prior.

Diamante, former rock star and lead singer of the group Zerosmith, will be assuming the role of Chief Communications Officer, and has only issued the following statement over the sudden acquisition: “He who owns the News owns the World, and Doffy is going to be King!”

Our reporters on the ground in Dressrosa, spoke to a few of the locals. “We’re all toys in his toybox!” said one Toy Soldier, outraged. He’s been an outspoken critic of the Monarch since his installment a little over a decade ago. Other citizens however, seem quite enthused over the prospect of Donquixote’s new ownership. “It’s about time! He’s the king of entertainment, I mean, look what he did with the Coliseum here! That’s just the kind of leadership Twitter needs! Gladiator style fights, blood sports in cyberspace!” said one man, who asked to remain anonymous.

The buyout is especially unexpected as, just last night, the multinational conglomerate, Baroque Works, headed by company President Sir Crocodile was in the offering stages of purchasing the troubled social media network. “We found a better venture to suit our needs,” was the only statement WENP was given. Could this be the start-up, Cross Guild, that Sir Crocodile announced earlier this year? The jury is still out.

Well readers, only time will tell how this move goes for the billionaire playboy. What new features would you like to see him implement on Twitter?
